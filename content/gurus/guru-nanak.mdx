# Guru Nanak

Guru Nanak (1469-1539) was the founder of Sikhism and the first of the ten Sikh Gurus. Born in the village of Rai Bhoi Ki Talwandi (now Nankana Sahib, Pakistan), he was a spiritual teacher, poet, and philosopher whose teachings laid the foundation for Sikhism.

## Early Life

Born to Mehta Kalu and Mata Tripta, Guru Nanak showed spiritual inclinations from an early age. He was known for his wisdom and compassion, often challenging social norms and religious orthodoxy.

## Spiritual Journey

At the age of 30, Guru Nanak disappeared for three days, emerging with a divine message: "There is no Hindu, there is no Muslim." This marked the beginning of his mission to spread the message of universal brotherhood and the oneness of God.

## Key Teachings

- The concept of Ik Onkar (One God)
- Equality of all human beings
- Rejection of caste system
- Importance of honest living and sharing
- Meditation on God's name
- Service to humanity

## Legacy

Guru Nanak's teachings continue to inspire millions worldwide. He established the foundation of Sikhism, emphasizing:
- Naam Japna (Meditation on God's name)
- Kirat Karni (Honest living)
- Vand Chakna (Sharing with others)

His message of universal brotherhood and equality remains relevant in today's world.
